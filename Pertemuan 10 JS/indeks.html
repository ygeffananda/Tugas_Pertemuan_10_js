PROJECT 1

The impact of marine debris on the environment and marine ecosystems

PROBLEMS

The impact of marine debris on the environment and marine ecosystems is huge and worrying. Marine debris can damage the marine environment by contaminating seawater, accumulating on the seafloor, disrupting marine animal life and damaging the marine ecosystem as a whole. In addition, marine debris can also affect human health through contamination of seafood and seawater which is processed into drinking water. Therefore, it is important to understand the negative impact of marine debris and take action to reduce waste production and keep the ocean clean.

SOLUTION

One of the solutions to overcome the problem of the impact of marine debris on the environment and marine ecosystems is to apply the 3R principles: Reduce, Reuse and Recycle. By reducing waste production, reusing items or materials that can still be used, and recycling waste, you can help reduce the amount of waste that enters the sea. In addition, it is also important to educate the public about the importance of keeping the environment clean and reducing the use of single-use plastics. The government also needs to implement policies that limit the use of single-use plastics, strengthen regulations on marine waste disposal, and increase law enforcement against violations related to the environment and marine debris.

PROJECT 2

The impact of marine debris on the environment and marine ecosystems

PROBLEMS

The destruction of coral reefs has become a critical global issue due to human activities, such as overfishing, pollution, and climate change. Coral reefs provide a habitat for numerous marine species, support the livelihoods of millions of people, and protect coastlines from erosion and storm damage. However, the damage to coral reefs is happening at an alarming rate, and it is estimated that around 50% of the world's coral reefs have already been lost or severely degraded.

SOLUTION

To address the problem of coral reef destruction, there are several measures that can be taken. One of the most important solutions is to reduce greenhouse gas emissions to mitigate the effects of climate change, which is causing ocean acidification and rising sea temperatures that are harming coral reefs. Another solution is to establish marine protected areas where fishing and other human activities are restricted to allow for the recovery of damaged coral reefs. Additionally, reducing pollution and unsustainable fishing practices can also help to preserve and protect coral reefs. It is crucial to work together with local communities and stakeholders.
